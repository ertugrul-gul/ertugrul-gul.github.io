01 — About

Decoding the mountain climate

I am a researcher at Hakkari University with a focus on climate variability, drought monitoring, and cryosphere dynamics in the Eastern Taurus and Hakkari mountains.

My primary tools are ERA5-Land reanalysis, SPEI/SPI drought indices, and Landsat/Sentinel-2 imagery to track the long-term evolution of glaciers and snowpack in the Cilo-Sat massif — one of Turkey's last significant glaciated areas.

I am also interested in downscaling global climate models to regional scales and communicating findings through accessible data visualization.

02 — Research

Areas of inquiry

❄

Glacier & Snow Dynamics

Tracking glacier area change and snowpack variability in the Cilo-Sat massif using multi-decadal satellite imagery and reanalysis temperature trends.

ERA5-Land Sentinel-2 Landsat
⟁

Drought & Climate Indices

Computing SPEI and SPI at multiple timescales to characterize drought frequency, duration, and severity across Hakkari Province from 1950 to present.

SPEI SPI Trend Analysis
◈

Temperature Anomaly Detection

Identifying warming trends and extreme heat events using ERA5-Land 2m temperature data with Mann-Kendall trend tests and Sen's slope estimation.

Mann-Kendall Sen's Slope Python
◎

Climate Data Visualization

Designing publication-quality figures for complex climatological datasets — spatial maps, temporal trend plots, anomaly heatmaps — for scientific and public audiences.
